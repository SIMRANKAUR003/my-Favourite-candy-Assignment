Ingredients

The ingredients for making gummy bear candies can vary depending on the recipe, but a basic recipe for gummy bears typically includes the following ingredients: Gelatin: This is the main ingredient that gives gummy bears their chewy texture. Gelatin is made from animal collagen and is available in both sheet and powdered forms. Water: Water is used to hydrate the gelatin and dissolve the other ingredients. Corn syrup: Corn syrup is used as a sweetener and to give gummy bears their characteristic shiny appearance. Sugar: Sugar is also used as a sweetener and to add bulk to the candy. Citric acid: Citric acid is used to give gummy bears a tart flavor. Flavoring: Gummy bears can be flavored with a variety of different extracts or flavorings, such as fruit extracts, vanilla, or mint.

Facts

Gummy bears were invented in 1922 by German candy maker Hans Riegel. He named the candy after his company, which was called Haribo. The original gummy bear flavors were raspberry, lemon, orange, strawberry, and pineapple. Gummy bears are made from a mixture of sugar, gelatin, flavorings, and colorings. The gelatin used to make gummy bears comes from animal collagen, which is extracted from the bones and connective tissue of pigs and cows. Gummy bears are a popular treat around the world, with variations and flavors unique to different countries.
